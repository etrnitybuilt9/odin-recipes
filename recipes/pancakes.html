Homemade Pancakes Recipe

Description

This recipe describes the ingredients and method for creating delicious homemade pancakes. There will be a list of ingredients in no particular order, and below that a list of steps to be taken as prescribed, in order of completion.

These pancakes should be tasty and suitable to consume as a meal if the ingredients and methodology are carried out as described below. The author makes no guarantee that the meal produced will be edible or delicious, as this recipe has not only been contrived, not actually cooked.

This is THE BEST recipe for Homemade Pancakes. They’re fluffy, flavorful, and easy to make from scratch. You’ll love the crispy golden edges and pillowy soft centers!

Ingredients

Dry Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 4 teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 pinch nutmeg

Wet Ingredients

  • 1 ¾ cups milk, I use 1%. Whole works too.
  • 4 tablespoons salted butter, Plus more for coating skillet.
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 ½ teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon honey
  • Nonstick cooking spray

Steps:

  1. Combine the dry ingredients in a large bowl until well-incorporated.
  2. Melt the butter and let it cool slightly.
  3. Create a well in the middle of the flour mixture. Add the milk, egg, vanilla extract, honey, and melted butter.
  4. Use a wire whisk to combine the batter but don’t overmix or the pancakes won’t be as soft. The batter should be thick, but if it’s too thick, add a small splash of milk.
  5. Preheat a nonstick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Once heated, reduce heat to medium-low and let it cool for 1 minute or so. Add a spray of nonstick cooking oil along with a sliver of butter and let it melt/foam.
  6. Ladle ¼ cup of batter over the butter. (If the edges aren’t quite circular, I use a silicone spatula to gently perfect the perimeter.)
  7. Cook until you see bubbles and dotted holes on the top, this takes about 2 minutes.
  8. Slide the spatula underneath the pancake and flip it. (Use a swift, uniform motion with your wrist, not your arm). Let the other side cook for about 30 seconds, you can use your spatula to peek underneath to ensure it’s set.
  9. Set aside and repeat with nonstick cooking spray, butter, and batter. Make minor adjustments to the heat (up or down), if needed throughout cooking.
  10. Serve with a pat of butter and warm syrup along with fruit if desired!
